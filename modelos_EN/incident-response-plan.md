Incident Response Plan

Version 1.0 — Template for Micro and Small Businesses

1. Purpose
Define a simple process to identify, record, and resolve information security incidents.

2. Types of Incidents
Common incidents include:

Phishing or fraud attempts

Malware, ransomware, or viruses

Unauthorized access

Lost or stolen equipment

Backup failures

Service interruptions

Accidental data exposure

5. Incident Response Process
5.1 Identification
Confirm what happened.

Collect screenshots, alerts, messages, or evidence.

Identify affected systems.

5.2 Containment
Disconnect affected equipment from the network if necessary.

Block compromised accounts.

Prevent the incident from spreading.

5.3 Eradication
Remove malware or malicious software.

Update systems and applications.

Reset affected passwords.

5.4 Recovery
Restore backups if needed.

Validate that systems are functioning normally.

Monitor for 48 hours to ensure stability.

5.5 Recording
Complete the incident log entry.

Store relevant evidence.

5.6 Post‑Incident Review
Identify the root cause.

Update procedures if needed.

Reinforce training with staff.

6. Review of the Plan
This plan is reviewed annually or after significant incidents.
