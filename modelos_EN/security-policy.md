Security Policy

Version 1.0 — Template for Micro and Small Businesses

1. Purpose
This policy defines the basic information security rules of the company, ensuring data protection, operational continuity, and risk reduction.

2. Scope
Applies to all employees, service providers, and equipment used for professional purposes.

3. General Principles
Information must be protected against unauthorized access.

The company uses simple and proportional security measures.

All employees share responsibility for security.

4. Access Management
Each user has individual credentials.

Strong passwords are mandatory.

MFA must be enabled whenever possible.

Old or unused accounts must be removed.

5. Equipment and Software
Only authorized software may be installed.

Outdated or unnecessary programs must be removed.

Operating systems and applications must remain updated.

The use of pirated software is prohibited.

6. Backups
Backups are performed automatically.

Recovery must be tested at least twice per year.

An offline or immutable copy must exist.

7. Email and Internet Use
Do not open suspicious attachments or links.

Always confirm payment requests or IBAN changes.

Installing unknown browser extensions is prohibited.

8. Mobile Devices
Devices must have PIN or biometric protection.

Encryption must be enabled when available.

Loss or theft must be reported immediately.

9. Incident Response
Any incident must be reported to the internal security contact.

The incident must be recorded using the incident log template.

Severe incidents may require contacting an external partner.

10. Training
All employees receive annual security awareness training.

Training includes phishing, passwords, backups, and good practices.

11. Policy Review
This policy is reviewed annually or whenever necessary.
